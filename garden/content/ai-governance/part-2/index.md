# What Can I Do About AI?

*Part 2 of 2 · Action guide · May 2026*

Twelve places to push the system — ranked by power, not visibility. Most "AI safety" activity clusters at the least effective tier. This guide maps where effort actually produces structural change, and who is best positioned to apply it.

---

## How to Use This Guide

This is a companion to [[../part-1/index|Part 1: Can AI Self-Govern?]], which explains why the AI development system behaves as it does. This guide takes the twelve leverage points from that analysis and makes them actionable.

The leverage points are ranked from weakest to most powerful. Higher-leverage interventions produce more durable structural change — but they also face more resistance, require more coordination, and take longer to move. There is no tier that makes this easy.

**The honest starting point:** Most "AI safety" public activity — petitions, social media pressure, awareness campaigns — clusters in the lowest tier. Real effect requires moving up. But moving up requires standing: institutional access, professional expertise, or coordination with others who have it.

**How to read each card:** Each card names the lever, explains what it actually is, identifies who can best move it, then describes how you encounter it and what changing it honestly does and doesn't do. Start with the tier that matches your current standing.

**The tier to prioritize:** High Leverage — Visibility and Enforceable Rules — is where individuals and institutions can create structural change in the next two to three years. It requires access: to journalists, to courts, to regulatory proceedings, or to workplaces deploying AI. The access itself is the constraint. These levers are available to more people than usually act on them.

**The highest ceiling:** Transformative levers have the highest ceiling and the longest timeline. They require coordinating actors who are actively competing with each other. Foundations for this are being laid now — and the decisions being made today about interpretability research and international frameworks will determine whether coordination becomes possible.

> [!tip] Tiers aren't exclusive
> Work in multiple tiers simultaneously. Visibility work (High Leverage) builds the evidence base that makes Coordination (Transformative) possible. Liability cases (High/Medium Leverage) change what company legal teams tell product teams without waiting for legislation. These levers interact — progress on one opens space on others.
