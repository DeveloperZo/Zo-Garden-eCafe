## Concept

Two diagrams explain the whole problem. First: why AI is accelerating faster than any single actor can control (DIA-002B — the capital ring). Second: why the system designed to catch that acceleration can't keep up (DIA-005 — the fast loop vs. the slow loop). Show both, explain both, land the structural conclusion. One idea, two visuals, 90 seconds.

## Production notes

- **Diagram readability:** Test both diagrams on an actual phone screen before recording. DIA-002B is dense — consider zooming to just the ring and arrows, with the five node labels in sequence. DIA-005 works better if the fast loop and slow loop use strongly contrasting colors (e.g., white/solid vs. gray/dashed), with the red sweep added last.
- **Pacing:** The capital ring section (0:07–0:40) should move at a steady clip — each node named quickly. The loops section (0:40–1:15) should slow down. This is where the structural argument lands. Give it room.
- **Tone:** Analytical, not alarmed. You're showing someone a diagram and explaining what it means, not warning them about a crisis.
- **On-screen text:** Minimal — the diagrams carry the visual weight. Resist the urge to add stat callouts on top of the diagrams.
