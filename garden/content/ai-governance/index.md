## The central question

> Can the companies building the most powerful technology in history be trusted to regulate themselves?

The structural answer — not based on the intentions of individuals, but on the loop structure of the system — is: not reliably. A fast reinforcing loop (capability → capital → compute) and a slow, under-resourced balancing loop (harm → oversight → correction) are mismatched in a way that drives behavior regardless of stated intentions. That's not a moral verdict. It's an observation about which loop is winning.

## Framework

Donella Meadows, *Thinking in Systems* (Chelsea Green, 2008). Twelve leverage points: pp. 145–165.

The key insight that organizes the analysis: when a reinforcing loop and a balancing loop are mismatched in speed, the system's behavior is governed by the faster loop — independent of the goals or values of the actors inside it.
